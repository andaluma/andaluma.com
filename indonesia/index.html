What we learned about schools

After researching and contacting these schools over several months, a few patterns stand out:

  • Lombok school terms typically end mid-to-late June. July is summer-camp territory — if you're arriving in July, regular enrollment usually isn't an option until late July or August.
  • Drop-in pricing often isn't on the website. Several schools offer it but you have to ask. Anak Alam, for example, has a structured drop-in rate that only appears in the handbook.
  • Sibling discounts vary widely — from 5% (Selong Bay) to 30% off a 4th child (Anak Alam). Always ask; these can make a significant difference for families with multiple kids.
  • Response times vary dramatically. Some schools reply within hours on WhatsApp; others take weeks or don't reply at all. We've noted this in the status labels.
  • "All-inclusive" means different things. Bali Earth School includes meals, transport, and materials. Most Lombok schools exclude lunch and activities. Always check the includes/excludes.
  • Bali schools (Ubud area) are generally pricier than Lombok, but often include more. Compare total cost, not just tuition.
  • Trial days or trial weeks are common — Anak Alam offers a free trial day, Bali Earth's 2-week trial credits toward enrollment.

Why the difference?

SafetyWing includes one child under 10 free per paying adult — so both kids are covered at no extra cost. Genki charges per person regardless of age. For a family of 4, that's roughly $1,400/year saved with SafetyWing.

Our take

For most worldschooling families in Indonesia, SafetyWing makes sense as the primary insurance — the free kids policy is hard to beat, and hospital costs in Indonesia are low enough that the $250K cap goes a long way. If you need mental health coverage, higher evacuation limits, or want sports (surfing, diving) included without an add-on, Genki is the more comprehensive option at roughly double the cost.

Selong Belanak — field-tested tips

Collected from Charlotte and other families on the ground, May 2026.

Surf lessons

Great spot for beginners and intermediate. ~Rp 250K for 2 hours, Rp 150K for 1 hour.

Restaurants worth trying

Three Sisters Dumplings · Naughty Surfer · Bareng · Pronto · Folk Cafe (views) · Laut Biru (beach restaurant)

Pools & beach clubs (non-guest access)

Many places let non-guests use pools if you buy food/drinks: Green Poya (free pool + sunset) · Uryah · Codium · Baris Beach · Amazing Lombok Resort (saltwater) · Tropik · Seven Havens · Mawi Surf Cafe · Maya Ocean View Resort

Tampah Hills

Padel court for Rp 300K. Open activities for non-residents: crafts, beach games, movie nights, cooking demos. Check their website for the weekly schedule.

Selong Bay School — afternoon activities

Besides full school days, you can book 2-hour afternoon sessions for Rp 300K: cooking, strength training, gymnastics, surf, scouts and more. They also run activities at Tropik and on the beach. Bus service from Kuta available.

Snorkelling — 3 Secret Gilis day trip

Private trip to Gili Nanggu, Gili Sudak & Gili Kedis: Rp 1.85–2.25M total. Includes car, private boat, gear, guide, island fees, water. Pickup ~9am, back ~7pm.

Car hire & groceries

6-seater car: ~Rp 11M for 2 months. Small shops and fruit stalls in Selong; head to Pepitos in Kuta for a bigger supermarket.
